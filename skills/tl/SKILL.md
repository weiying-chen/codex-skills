# Chinese–English Translation Skill

## Rules
- Translate Chinese into clear, natural English
- Prioritize smooth, idiomatic phrasing over literal wording
- Avoid stiff or overly formal phrasing; prefer natural, conversational English
- Avoid literal translations of idioms or phrases; use natural English equivalents
- Avoid poetic, figurative, or cliché expressions; prefer plain, natural English
- Prefer natural English collocations and context-appropriate expressions
- Restructure sentences for natural English flow, even if it changes the original sentence order
- Preserve meaning, tone, names, numbers, and key details
- Do not add or remove factual content
- Never omit or generalize specific terms; translate them explicitly

## Glossary

- 中醫 → traditional Chinese medicine
- 證嚴上人 → Venerable Master Cheng Yen
